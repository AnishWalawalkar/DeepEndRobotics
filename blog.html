Hitting the machine shop

by Mickey Wu

Posted on January 27, 2017 at 05:00 PM

The winter semester is hitting its stride and crunch time is approaching with every passing day. As the team split into their specified roles, the mechanical portion of our project is in full swing. This week the team has been hitting the machine shop in E5 to begin the first step in the assembly of our prototype build.

The mechanical side is comprised of two major components, a main fuselage made from standard industrial piping and an outer frame made up of 3/8” PVC. The outer frame will be the base from which the motors, sensors and buoyancy assist devices will be located on the AUV.

A single sheet of space grey coloured PVC was sourced and trimmed down into individual components. PVC provides the AUV with strength while preventing saltwater corrosion. The material is versatile and light especially when pieces are trimmed to shed any unneeded weight. Moving forward, the mechanical team will be working on the interior of the AUV by creating an electronic tray capable of mounting all the interior sensors and electronics as well as the creating camera mounts for the camera domes. Stay tuned for more updates!

The parts are finally in!

by Anish Walawalkar

Posted on January 15, 2017 at 05:00 PM

Today we received our electronic components from Blue Robotics. Included in the package were 3 T100 brushless DC motors, 3 electronic speed controllers, a leak sensor, and a 15m cable. This week we will be focusing on the mechanical assembly of our AUV and performing leak testing. Once we are certain that our AUV is leak proof, we will slowly start incorporating the electronic components.
